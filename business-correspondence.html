Business Correspondence

In this assignment we were asked to read through an annual report and write a memo assessing the report. I chose to base my report on Microsoft’s 2020 annual report and evaluated the first three sections.

To: Professor Potluri
From: CJ Spagnolia
Date: May 13, 2021
Subject: Short Report on Microsoft’s Annual Report

I am sharing what I have learned from the first few sections of Microsoft’s Annual Report of 2020 as well as my insights on how the company fared during the height of the pandemic and how they managed to help others prosper in a time of uncertainty. I will be discussing three sections from the report: shareholder's letter, financial analysis, and business.

Shareholder's Letter
Microsoft’s annual report outlines how despite the global pandemic hurting most other industries, the technology sector was able to produce profit and growth. Most of the Shareholder Letter is focused on how they pushed through and showed resilience by highlighting portions of their business that have seen success. The information in the first section is laid out in a cohesive way that is to the point while still being full of information that shareholders would find important.

There is only one thing that I think this section is missing. The letter places a large focus on Azure, their cloud computing platform, and not much time is spent on any other part of their operations. It would benefit the investors if there was more information as to what the company is doing besides Azure as they will have more background in their investment.

Financial Analysis
The Financial Review section backs up their earlier claims that the technology sector was able to handle the pandemic well. 2020 was one of the most profitable years to date. There is a highlights section that is a nice touch for those who do not want to read the whole thing but still want a view into how the company’s finances were. The section as a whole is short and to the point.

The section could be made easier to follow. There are a lot of tables and a single graph with little to no context as to why this year was financially different from other years. The graph’s key could be misread as “6/15” as June 15th instead of the intended June 2015. This could be easily rectified by adding the first two numbers to the year.

Business
The third portion of the report is dedicated to outlining their future business ambitions. A large part of their plans for the future are about their Azure cloud computing, as it is a major source of revenue for the company, and Artificial Intelligence. The section overall is a lot more detailed than the previous two sections with specific information as to what they plan to do in the near and distant future.

The only thing in this section that I can recommend is increasing the contrast between subsections and differentiating between topics in the subsections. As it is right now the main way to figure out what topic you are reading is by looking at the top right corner where it says clearly what section you are in.

In summary, Microsoft has been able to be more profitable than ever despite the pandemic ruining most other companies finances. The report itself is laid out in a legible and logical fashion with sparse use of tables and very limited graphs. Overall, if there were more graphs and tables to drive home points made, the readability would be higher.
